How the prediction works

Summary: Papers are ranked by predicted (or current) citation count.

  1. Data: Citation network (who cites whom) + paper year and topic (group).
  2. LTCM (when installed): Fit a Long-Term Citation Model on training years; predict future citations per paper; rank by predicted count.
  3. Fallback: If LTCM is not available, rank by current in-degree (citations received so far).
  4. Output: List of papers ordered by impact, with education topic labels.

Reference: Wang, Song & Barabási, Science 2013 (Long-Term Citation Model).
